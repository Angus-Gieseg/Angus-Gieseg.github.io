Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

At my previous work we had a culture of "she'll be right" when designing some structural elements such as beams and columns. The "she'll be right" I precieved is called the "engineering judgement". When someone has years of experience the usually don't need to do all the nitty gritty calculations, but if someones lazy and over confident this could lead to real issues. This scared the hell out of me as a fresh graduate. I talked with my manager and discussed designing a SMATH sheet to automatically size beams to replace the outdated spreadsheets they were using. I identified through previous jobs it was easier for an engineerer to review the code for an SMATH sheet to check the assumptions and formulas over using an excel spreadsheet which are inherently hard to reverse engineer. The project took me a full week to build and QA with the technical director, what I later found out was Engineers often guess sizes of beams and end up over-engineering the sizes so in the end it usually works out fine. Only a few of the engineers ended up using the new SMATH sheet while others preferred to rely on their "engineering judgement".

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

The culture had its pro's and con's like many places, we had a great manager who showed an interest working with the team and building mutual respect. Given ownership of projects much earlier than working at other firms and they made and effort to have social events with the team. The downside was how overworked the team was with little collaberation. The teams were siloed and followed a waterfall approach to project management. If I was to stay a structural Engineer in NZ they would probably still be one of the best places to work at. I was incredibly thankful for the support and time invested in me, primarily by the technical director. The technical director encouraged me to feed my curiousity and grow a confidence in being diligent about in design and verification.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

Most recently there has been tension within my Univeristy group project. I chose to take the role of Structural Engineer which is has the highest workload compared to 3 waters and infrastructure. I initially didn't want to take the position as I was already working fulltime and still work in a startup. I saw the rest of the team didn't feel confident and so I volunteered as I had experience already doing similar work outside of University. This meant I could lead by example and had some weight to delegate additional work between the team. The issue that arose was the geotechnical engineer already had far more work than the waste wate engineer and they both needed to do discuss in their reports ecological & archaeological implications. We made a compromise during a meeting to have the waste water engineer complete a more detailed report that the geotechnical engineer could pull from. Being a third party between the two meeting I had no vested interest other than getting the work complete in a timely manner which I believe helped us reach a conclusion around the work expectations and deadlines.
